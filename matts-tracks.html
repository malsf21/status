Matt's Notes

September/October 2016

Carly Rae Jepsen still keeps the spotlight, as well as Rae Sremmurd and Vince Staples. Kanye and Halsey are making a run for the top: we'll see. But, you know, I still like Carly Rae Jepsen a lot.

August/September 2016

For that last little part of summer (and a bit of the school year), I shifted my listening habits a bit away from rap: you'll see MGMT, lots and lots of Carly Rae Jepsen (she's amazing), and CL. Joining them are lots of Chance the Rapper, Rae Sremmund, and Kaiydo.

July/August 2016

There's a huge difference in between this month's list and June/July. There's a lot, and I mean a lot, of Logic; Super Mario World and Flexecution has been some pretty awesome workout music while I was at SHAD. Most of the other recurring songs are rap: Champions by G.O.O.D Music, Pop Style by Drake, and Random by G-Eazy come back again (mostly, again, as rap workout music).

June/July 2016

This is the first time I've done this project, and I'm not sure how it's going to turn out! If you look at the choices of songs available here, there's a lot, and a lot, of hip-hop. Recurring artists include Drake, Logic, Kanye, and Chance the Rapper: they reflect my tastes for the month, especially with Chance's new project Coloring Book, Drake's chart-topping One Dance, Kanye's extensive catalog, and a deep-down fanboy love of Logic.
